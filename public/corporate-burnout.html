The Data

You're Not Broken. The System Is.

Burnout is not a personal weakness. It is a documented, measurable epidemic — and it's getting worse.

+32%
Burnout Reviews Year-over-Year
Glassdoor reviews mentioning burnout increased 32% year-over-year in early 2025, reaching the highest level since tracking began in 2016 — and 50% higher than pre-pandemic. Burnout employees rate their employers 2.68/5, compared to 3.61/5 for those who don't mention burnout.
Glassdoor Work-Life Trends Report, 2025
65%
Feel Stuck in Their Role
Glassdoor's Work-Life Trends report found that 65% of employees feel stuck in their current roles. Employee satisfaction across industries has been declining. "As workers feel stuck, pent-up resentment boils under the surface and employee disengagement rises."
Glassdoor Work-Life Trends, 2025
1 in 4
Americans Burned Out Before 30
A quarter of Americans report experiencing burnout before age 30. Gen Z and Millennials are hitting burnout earlier than any previous generation, driven by financial pressure, student debt, always-on digital work culture, and shrinking career advancement opportunities.
Workplace mental health research, 2025
70%
Of C-Suite Executives Want Out
70% of C-suite executives themselves feel so overwhelmed they want to quit for roles that better support their well-being. When even the people at the top of the ladder want off, it tells you something fundamental about the ladder itself.
Executive wellness survey, 2025
5-yr low
Voluntary Quit Rate
The voluntary quit rate has fallen to a five-year low — not because workers are happy, but because the job market gives them nowhere to go. People are staying in jobs they hate because unemployment in a contracting market is worse. This is not loyalty. It is captivity.
Bureau of Labor Statistics JOLTS data
82%
At Risk of Burnout This Year
82% of employees are at risk of burnout in 2026 according to Fortune/Yahoo Finance research. The majority — not a minority — are in the same situation you're in.
Burnout is not a sign that something is wrong with you. It is a sign that something is wrong with the system — and that the system's solution (more wellness apps, more unlimited PTO you can never use) is not going to fix it.
Why It Stays This Way

Your Employer's Solution to Burnout Is a Wellness App.
That's Not Going to Cut It.

The structural forces driving burnout are getting stronger — not weaker.

"They're giving you more work, not more people."

As companies use AI and budget pressure to reduce headcount, the workload does not shrink. It gets redistributed to the remaining employees. You are doing the work of 1.5 people and being paid for 0.95 of one. The math is not going to get better until you change it yourself.

"The career ladder has fewer rungs."

Companies are flattening organizational structures and eliminating middle management layers. The promotions you were waiting for are disappearing. The path from where you are to where you want to be is being removed while you are standing on it — and there is no announcement when it happens.

"Burnout is the mechanism that keeps you stuck."

When you are burned out, you do not have the energy to start a side project, learn new skills, network, or plan an exit. Burnout is not just a symptom — it is the mechanism that keeps you in place. The longer you stay in it without changing something fundamental, the harder it becomes to change anything at all.

"Wellness programs don't solve structural problems."

Companies are investing in meditation apps, mental health days, and wellness stipends. These address symptoms while ignoring the cause: too much work, too little pay, too few advancement opportunities, and too little autonomy. You cannot meditate your way out of a broken system. The fix has to be structural — on your side.
