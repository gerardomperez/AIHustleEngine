The Hard Truth

Why Waiting Is the Most Expensive Decision You Can Make

There is no "ideal time" to act on this. Every month you wait, the math gets worse.

"AI doesn't plateau — it compounds."

Every major AI model released in the past 18 months has been significantly more capable than the one before it. The tasks AI couldn't do last year, it can do this year. The tasks it struggles with today, it will handle by next year. Your job security erodes with every model update — and the updates are coming faster than ever.

"Companies have financial incentive to replace you."

Corporate leadership is under constant pressure to reduce costs and increase margins. AI offers a way to do both simultaneously. An AI tool that costs $200/month and works 24/7 will always be chosen over a $75,000/year employee — the moment the AI can perform at 80% of the employee's capability. That threshold is being crossed in industry after industry.

"The job market is already frozen."

Even for people who still have jobs, the fallback plan of "I'll just find another one" is breaking down. Job postings are declining, applicant counts per posting are exploding, and many companies have implemented quiet hiring freezes while they figure out their AI strategy. The safety net of "I can always get another corporate job" is fraying.

"Entry-level is disappearing first — and mid-level is next."

Companies are eliminating entry-level positions because AI can now handle the tasks those roles were designed for. This creates a pipeline problem: fewer entry-level workers being trained means companies will increasingly use AI for mid-level tasks too — not because AI is better, but because there is no one being groomed to fill those seats.
