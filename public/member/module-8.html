Lesson 1

Revenue Stacking

30 min

Multiple income streams that complement

Getting to $10,000/month is a systems problem, not a talent problem. Most AI entrepreneurs who plateau at $3,000-$5,000/month have the skills to earn significantly more ‚Äî they're just spending their time wrong. This lesson introduces the scale framework. We cover the three leverage points for scaling: offer leverage (charging more for the same time), capacity leverage (delivering more without proportionally more time), and distribution leverage (reaching more prospects without proportionally more effort). You'll map your current business against this framework and identify the 2-3 changes that would have the biggest impact.
I talk to freelancers making $4,000/month who feel like they're on a ceiling. When I look at their business, the ceiling isn't talent or opportunity ‚Äî it's structure. They're doing $50/hour work and $200/hour work in the same day with no system to separate them.

Key Takeaways

  • The 3 leverage points: Offer, capacity, and distribution leverage
  • Time audit: How to identify what to cut, delegate, and automate
  • The offer stack: How to increase average client value without more clients
  • Delegation framework: What to outsource first for maximum leverage
  • Revenue milestone map: What needs to happen at $3K, $5K, $10K, $20K/month

Action Items

  • Complete a time audit: Track everything for 3 days and categorize by leverage
  • Identify your top 3 time-wasters that could be delegated or automated
  • Recalculate your effective hourly rate across all activities
  • Build your revenue milestone map with specific actions at each stage

Lesson 2

Team & Delegation

35 min

When and how to hire

Building a team is the multiplier that takes most solopreneurs from $10K to $30K+ per month ‚Äî but it's also where many go wrong. Hiring too early, hiring wrong roles, or failing to document processes before delegating are all common costly mistakes. We cover the lean team model for AI businesses: which roles to hire first, how to write job posts that attract quality candidates, how to vet AI skills specifically, and how to build management systems that don't require your constant presence. You'll have your first hiring post written and a role description ready by the end of this lesson.
The first person you hire should take something you hate doing off your plate ‚Äî not something you love. When you delegate drains, you free up energy for the work that drives revenue.

Key Takeaways

  • The lean team model: Maximum output with minimum management overhead
  • First hire framework: Operations VA vs. delivery assistant vs. salesperson
  • How to evaluate AI skills in candidates who claim them
  • Onboarding a remote team member using SOPs and AI-assisted training
  • Management without micromanagement: Weekly systems that keep things on track

Action Items

  • Define the first role you'll hire for and what they'll take off your plate
  • Write your hiring post using the Hiring Post Writer prompt
  • Build a 30-day onboarding plan for the role
  • Create the SOP for the first task you'll delegate

Lesson 3

Operational Excellence

30 min

Systems that scale

Recurring revenue is the foundation of a stable, scalable AI business. One-time project work creates feast-or-famine cycles that are stressful and hard to plan around. Recurring revenue through retainers, subscriptions, or memberships creates financial stability that lets you invest in growth. This lesson covers the full recurring revenue strategy: converting project clients to retainers, designing retainer offers clients want to stay in, and building subscription products. We also cover the metrics that matter: churn rate, lifetime value, and MRR. You'll design your first retainer or subscription offer before this lesson is over.
I made $2,400 one month from project work. The next month, $1,800. The month after, $4,100. That rollercoaster is exhausting. Now I have $9,500/month in locked-in retainer income before I wake up on the 1st. The path to get there is repeatable.

Key Takeaways

  • Project vs. retainer: Why retainers are better for almost every AI business
  • Retainer design: Creating an offer clients renew without being asked
  • The conversion conversation: How to transition a project client to a retainer
  • Churn prevention: Early warning signs and how to address them
  • MRR milestone planning: What recurring revenue milestones unlock

Action Items

  • Design your retainer offer: What's included, what's excluded, monthly price
  • Write your retainer proposal using the Retainer Client Proposal prompt
  • Identify 3 current/past project clients who would benefit from ongoing support
  • Calculate: How many retainers at your target price equals your income goal?
