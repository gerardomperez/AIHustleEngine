Lesson 1

High-Demand AI Services

20 min

What businesses are paying for right now

Your offer is the foundation of your AI services business. A weak offer means endless objections and clients who don't see your value. A strong offer means premium pricing, easy sales conversations, and clients who refer you. This lesson teaches you how to build the latter. We cover the anatomy of a compelling service offer: the transformation promised, the target client, the proof you can deliver, and the risk reversal that removes hesitation. You'll walk away with a draft offer statement ready to test this week.
I ask every person I coach the same question when they're stuck: What does your client's life look like 30 days after they hire you? Most people answer by describing their deliverables. That's wrong. The answer should describe a transformation. That shift changes everything.

Key Takeaways

  • The 4 components every high-converting service offer needs
  • How to shift from 'I provide X service' to 'I help Y achieve Z result'
  • Offer naming: Why your service name is a marketing decision
  • Risk reversal: How to make it easy to say yes
  • The difference between a feature list and an outcome promise

Action Items

  • Write your one-sentence offer statement using the template provided
  • Test your offer on 3 people outside your industry ‚Äî can they explain it back?
  • Identify 3 case studies or examples that prove your offer delivers results
  • Draft your first service package (starter/core/premium) with clear deliverables

Lesson 2

Pricing Your Services

25 min

How to charge premium rates for AI work

Pricing is where most freelancers leave the most money on the table. Not because they price too high ‚Äî but because they undercharge, use hourly rates that punish efficiency, and fail to anchor pricing to value created. This lesson changes that. We cover value-based pricing in depth: how to identify the economic value of the outcome you create, and how to present pricing that makes the premium feel obvious and logical. By the end, you'll have a pricing structure that reflects the real value you provide.
I charged $50 an hour when I started. At that rate, the fact that AI let me work 3x faster made me 3x less money, not more. When you price on outcomes instead of time, AI efficiency becomes pure profit.

Key Takeaways

  • Why hourly pricing is the enemy of AI-powered business growth
  • How to calculate the economic value of your service's outcome
  • The 3-tier pricing model and why clients almost always choose the middle
  • Price anchoring: Make your real price seem like a bargain
  • How to raise prices with existing clients without losing them

Action Items

  • Calculate the ROI your service creates for a typical client
  • Rebuild your pricing using outcome-based value instead of time
  • Create your 3-tier package with clear differentiation at each level
  • Practice saying your price out loud without qualifying or apologizing

Lesson 3

Getting Your First Clients

30 min

Proven outreach strategies

Getting your first client is the most important milestone in your AI services business ‚Äî more important than your tool stack, your website, or your niche. The first client proves the model, gives you a case study, and creates confidence to sell to the next one. We cover a battle-tested outreach system: identifying 20 warm prospects, crafting a message that gets a response, running a discovery call, and closing without feeling pushy. All with zero advertising budget. You'll leave with an outreach plan, a list of 20 prospects, and a first message draft ready to send today.
Stop waiting until your website is perfect, your LinkedIn is optimized, and you have a case study. None of that matters for your first client. What matters is sending 20 targeted messages to the right people with the right offer. That's it.

Key Takeaways

  • Why your first client should come from warm outreach, not cold prospecting
  • How to identify 20 high-probability prospects from your existing network
  • The 3-message outreach sequence that books discovery calls
  • Discovery call framework: Diagnose the problem and present your solution
  • How to close without pressure using the consultative close

Action Items

  • List 20 people in your network who could use your service or know someone who could
  • Draft your 3-message outreach sequence using the Cold Email Sequence prompt
  • Send your first 5 outreach messages today
  • Book your first discovery call within 7 days
