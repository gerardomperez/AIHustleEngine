Lesson 1

The 5 AI Income Models

20 min

Services, products, content, agencies, and arbitrage

Before you spend a single hour building skills or creating offers, you need a strategic framework for evaluating which AI income opportunity is right for you specifically. This lesson gives you that framework. We examine the 5 primary AI income categories: services (fastest to first dollar), products (scalable but slower), agency (high ceiling), content (compounding), and automation (high value, specialized). You'll leave knowing exactly which 1-2 income streams to pursue first.
Everyone wants to know: What's the fastest way to make money with AI? The honest answer is: it depends. But I've identified a scoring system that gives a highly reliable answer for any individual situation in about 10 minutes.

Key Takeaways

  • The 5 AI income categories and their risk/reward profiles
  • How to score each income stream against your current situation
  • Why starting with services is almost always the right first move
  • The sequencing strategy: Which income stream unlocks the next
  • Common mistakes people make when choosing an income stream

Action Items

  • List your top 5 professional skills and match them to AI income opportunities
  • Identify your #1 income stream to pursue for the next 90 days

Lesson 2

Skill-AI Matching

25 min

Map your existing skills to AI opportunities

Services are the fastest path to AI income. There's no product to build, no audience to grow, no complex funnel to create. You identify a problem, solve it using AI, and someone pays you. This lesson shows you how to find what people will pay for right now. We cover demand research techniques: mining job boards, Reddit, freelance platforms, and LinkedIn to find AI services with genuine demand and pricing power. You'll leave with a prioritized shortlist of 3-5 specific services you could offer starting this week.
Here's the dirty secret about building a services business: most people skip research entirely. They decide what they want to offer based on what sounds exciting ‚Äî and then they're confused when nobody buys. Research first, build second. Every time.

  • The AI efficiency test: How to identify services where AI gives you an unfair advantage
  • Pricing research: How to find what the market actually pays for AI services
  • The difference between services people want and services people pay for
  • How to validate a service idea before investing time in it

Lesson 3

Market Research with AI

30 min

Validate demand before building

Digital products are the holy grail of AI income: create once, sell forever. But the path from idea to profitable product is littered with people who built things nobody wanted. This lesson shows you how to validate, design, and position digital products with genuine market demand. We cover the three fastest digital product types to create with AI: templates and frameworks, guides and reports, and micro-tools. You'll have a validated product idea before this lesson is over.
I want to reframe how you think about digital products. Most people think of them as big, complex things that take months to build. But some of the highest-converting digital products are 5-page PDF checklists that took 4 hours to make and sell for $27 a pop.

Key Takeaways

  • The 3 fastest digital products to create with AI assistance
  • How to validate demand before building anything
  • Pricing strategy for digital products at different price points
  • Distribution channels that work best for AI-created products
  • How to create a publishable digital product in under 72 hours

Action Items

  • Validate your top idea by finding 10 people currently spending money in this space
  • Outline your first digital product (can be a simple PDF or template pack)
  • Research 3 competitors selling similar products and analyze their positioning
